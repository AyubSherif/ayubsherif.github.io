About Me

I’m a Data Analyst II at Loeb Electric, where my role has grown into a hybrid of data analyst, data engineer, and data scientist. This evolution came from consistently pushing boundaries and delivering results that go beyond expectations. To meet these expanding demands, I began a Master’s in Data Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. I also hold a BS/MS degree in Engineering Physics and Nuclear Engineering from The Ohio State University.
My major projects include:

  • Building Power BI reporting infrastructure including a centralized Fabric SQL Warehouse, and standardized departmental Power BI Semantic Models and reports (Sales, Inventory, Purchasing, etc.).
  • ML-powered Product Taxonomy Classifier to replace manual product categorization
  • Warehouse Utilization Forecasting to guide replenishment and space planning
  • Developing (In progress) a warehouse optimization tool to minimize picking/receiving travel distance

Previously, at Serva Energy, I built a real-time signal denoising filter for high activity radiation data using an innovative template matching method.
Whether it’s deploying machine learning models in production, visualizing actionable insights in Power BI, or solving physics-based problems, I bring scientific thinking and relentless curiosity to every project.
I’m always learning and I love teaching—my team jokingly refers to it as “Ayub University.” I believe in raising the bar—not just for myself, but for the teams around me.
On the topic of learning, check out my very cool Educational tool, or navigate to the Projects section to see some of my work in action.
